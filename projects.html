SMUD Solar Regatta 2016

For the competition we decided to utilize a pulse width modulator for motor control; intermediately from the battery to the motor. This allowed for lower power dissipation from the PWM's low impedance and duty cycle, while also keeping the motors voltage constant.

After a few modificatoins we were able to constructed a mount for the PWM and Solar 80 charge controller.

We utilized an old exercise bike as the load for motor testing. Under this load the motor drew 19A at about 2700 RPM. Yet during the race the 12V 15A motor was being burned to $#*! by almost 30A (we still managed to do well).

Each solar panel supplied 30 volts in direct sunlight according to our measurements. According to Bosch's specs the solar panels were rated at 8A, 34V, and 250W each - assuming a very sunny day!
